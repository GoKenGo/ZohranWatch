What is Zohran Mamdani's Platform?

Zohran Mamdani won the NYC mayoralty on a bold working-class platform that rejects decades of failed neoliberal governance. His campaign centered on universal programs that benefit everyone—not means-tested scraps—and a willingness to directly challenge the real estate developers, corporate landlords, and Wall Street interests that have dominated city politics for too long.

This is what democracy looks like when working people, not billionaires, set the agenda.

Key Components

Mamdani's platform is built on four core principles of working-class power:

Universal Social Programs: Free buses, universal childcare, and public grocery stores—services that strengthen our communities and prove government can work for regular people, not just the wealthy.

Housing as a Human Right: Rent freeze to stop displacement, eviction protections with teeth, and 100,000 units of permanently affordable housing built through public development—not luxury condos for foreign investors.

Economic Justice: A $30 minimum wage by 2030, progressive taxation that makes the rich pay their fair share, and corporate accountability to end the race to the bottom.

Democratic Governance: Transparent budgeting with participatory decision-making, and a clean break from the corruption and incompetence of the Adams era.

Policy Areas

We're tracking implementation across seven critical areas where working New Yorkers have been failed by austerity politics:

Housing: Rent freeze, eviction enforcement, 100,000 affordable units. Because housing is a human right, not a commodity for speculation.

Transit: Free buses starting with working-class neighborhoods, expanded pilots. Public transportation should be free at the point of use, like roads for cars.

Childcare: Universal childcare for all families, living wages for care workers. Families shouldn't have to choose between work and raising kids.

Food: Publicly-owned grocery stores, elimination of food deserts. No neighborhood should be abandoned by capitalism.

Wages: $30 minimum wage by 2030. If wages had kept pace with productivity, this would already be the floor.

Taxes: Progressive taxation, corporate tax reform. The wealthy have been freeloading for decades while our schools and hospitals crumble.

Governance: Transparent budget, participatory decision-making. Democracy means the people have power, not just a vote every four years.

Why This Matters

Why We Support This Platform:

  • Centers working-class New Yorkers who've been abandoned by both parties
  • Provides universal programs that build solidarity, not means-tested welfare that divides us
  • Directly confronts real estate and corporate power instead of negotiating surrender
  • Draws on proven international examples—social housing in Vienna, free transit in Luxembourg
  • Represents an actual alternative to the "progressive austerity" that's failed us
  • Fights for the multiracial working class, not identity politics without material change

Who Opposes This (And Why):

  • Real estate developers who profit from the housing crisis
  • Corporate landlords extracting wealth from working families
  • Wall Street firms that want to privatize public services
  • Centrist politicians invested in "pragmatic" surrender to business interests
  • Media outlets owned by billionaires threatened by progressive taxation
  • State legislators beholden to the same donors who funded Adams

Our Mission: This tracker exists to hold Mayor Mamdani accountable to the working-class movement that elected him. We won't accept excuses, backroom deals, or the "politics of the possible" that always seems possible for Wall Street but impossible for working people.

Our Bias: We're explicitly on the side of tenants over landlords, workers over bosses, and the multiracial working class over the ruling class. We believe these policies are good and should be implemented. We're tracking them because accountability builds power.

About This Tracker

Zohran Watch was created by organizers and activists who believe electoral politics only matters if we hold elected officials accountable after the votes are counted.

Inspired by the Project 2025 Observer and rooted in NYC's proud tradition of labor organizing and tenant activism, this tracker exists because we know power concedes nothing without a demand.

We're not journalists pretending to be neutral. We're partisans for the working class. We built this tool because we believe in these policies, we fought for this victory, and we'll fight to make sure these promises become reality.

This is an independent project, not affiliated with any campaign or organization. We answer to the people, not politicians. If Mamdani sells out, we'll be the first to call it out.

Solidarity forever. The people united will never be defeated.
